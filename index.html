Easter Bunnies Don't Wear Shoes

November 4th, 2023

If you know me at all, you might be wondering why am I even doing this? It's a fair question, and one I asked myself as well recently. Why am I doing this?.. Part of me has been wanting to have my own website for a long time now, but I think the reason is much deeper than that, one that really gets to the heart of who I am as a person.

"Dad, why is the Easter Bunny wearing gym shoes?" I recall asking my dad this question at a young age when he took me to go see the Easter Bunny one year. Come to think of it, I don't recall ever going to see the Easter Bunny again after that memory, so I wonder if I spoiled it for the other children.. I often recall this memory because I believe it really captures a fundamental aspect of who I am. I have always been good at recognizing patterns, and consequently things that don't fit them. I've always been curious and loved to ponder the intricate workings of our universe, even the seemingly mundane things. I was that child who kept asking adults why until the gave up answering or maybe came to the realization that maybe they didn't actually know why themselves. Of course, some adults probably didn't want to admit to me that they didn't know, more likely it was "because that's just the way it is" or "because I said so". But for me I I wasn't so interested in sticking it to them as I was fascinated that even someone 3 times my age couldn't tell me why the sky was blue. I mean, not knowing the "why" for something that we look at every single say must mean that there is a complicated reason that is difficult to explain to a child after all. And as it turns out, there is! As for Myself, I never *really* learned why the sky was blue until I learned about Rayleigh and Raman scattering in my college classes - the physics phenomena that describe the way light scatters and the tendency for light of smaller wavelengths such as blue to be scattered more. It was there, sitting in my college physical chemistry class that I realized it's no wonder so many adults would eventually get stumped of you keep asking why - the level of complexity of some of the most mundane things we often take for granted is astounding. And it is that same complexity and my desire to make sense of it that drives me to do so much of what I do.

Do I want to be a web developer? No, not really. But do I have this insatiable desire to just try and see if I can figure out (knowing basically nothing about web development or design) how to build a website from absolute scratch? You bet! I can't even really explain it, maybe it's because in our increasingly complex world where the way things work seems to be getting more abstract every day (come on, how does a computer actually work, they are basically magic) or maybeit is just because I like to take on the challenge and see if I can learn something new. Theres something about building things from scratch is just immensely satisfying and rewarding to me. So here is my feeble attempt at building a website in 10x the time it would take for an actual web dev to do it and probably for something that looks 10x worse.. but I will be able to call it mine and hopefully learn a lot along the way!
